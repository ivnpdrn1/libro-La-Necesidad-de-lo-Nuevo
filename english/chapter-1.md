Chapter 1 — The Accident

Mateo did not remember the exact moment he lost his footing.
He remembered the edge. The height. The brief hesitation before his body moved forward without asking for permission.
What he did not remember was the decision.
The fall was real.
The air rushed past him with the same indifferent speed it always had, and the ground rose with the same certainty.
Nothing in the world altered its behavior.
Everything followed its course.
Everything except what came after.
When Mateo opened his eyes, the first thing he noticed was not pain.
It was continuity.
The world had not paused.
Cars moved. Voices carried. A door closed somewhere in the distance.
Everything continued.
He stayed still for a moment.
Not out of fear.
Out of expectation.
Pain should arrive.
He waited.
It came.
But not enough.
Mateo inhaled slowly.
The sensation was there—but it did not impose itself.
He moved his hand.
Then his arm.
No resistance.
He sat up.
Someone spoke nearby.
He did not process the words.
Only the tone.
Something was wrong.
Or should have been.
Mateo stood.
Too quickly.
Nothing stopped him.
No correction.
That was the first fracture.
Not in the body.
In expectation.
The world had not broken.
It had failed to correct.
And in that failure, something shifted.
Not loudly.
Not completely.
But enough.
