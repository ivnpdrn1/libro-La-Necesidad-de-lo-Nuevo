
Chapter 10 — Love Without Limits

Lucía did not think she was doing anything wrong.
There was no sense of crossing a line, no internal signal demanding correction. What she felt was not excess. It was clarity—uncomfortable, but precise.
The problem was not what she felt.
It was what she was no longer willing to accept.
Daniel had not responded.
He had read the message. The confirmation was there, silent and definitive.
But there was no reply.
Not immediately.
Not later.
Before, that would have been enough.
Silence had weight. It marked distance. It indicated direction.
Now it did not.
Lucía set the phone down and did not look at it again for several minutes.
Not as discipline.
As a decision.
She stood and walked to the window.
The city continued.
Lights.
Movement.
Structure.
Nothing suggested change.
But she no longer interpreted those signals in the same way.
She thought about their last conversation.
It had not been an argument.
That was part of the problem.
He said he needed time.
She said she understood.
And in that exchange, something that would once have moved toward a defined outcome—continuation or rupture—remained suspended.
No closure.
No direction.
No consequence is strong enough to settle it.
Lucía rested her forehead lightly against the glass.
Cold.
Real.
That still held.
Everything else felt less certain.
She returned to the table, picked up the phone, and opened the conversation.
She read her last message.
It was not emotional.
Not excessive.
It was clear.
And yet it had produced nothing.
Before, silence from the other person would have forced something—acceptance, withdrawal, reorganization.
Now it did not.
Lucía began typing.
Stopped.
Deleted the words.
The question was not enough.
She typed again.
Short.
Direct.
Sent.
Seconds passed.
Then more.
Nothing.
Lucía placed the phone down again and breathed.
She was not anxious.
That, too, was new.
Waiting had lost weight.
And that did not comfort her.
She sat and closed her eyes briefly, trying to locate the feeling.
It was not abandonment.
Not loss.
It was something in between.
A relationship that had not ended but was no longer moving.
That did not make sense.
Lucía opened her eyes.
If time no longer resolved things, why accept that they would end on their own?
The phone vibrated.
Daniel.
She picked it up.
“I’m fine. I just need space.”
The phrase was familiar.
Too familiar.
Before, it would have been enough.
Now it was not.
Lucía did not respond immediately.
She stood and walked slowly across the apartment.
Two cups on the table.
A book he had left.
His jacket is on the chair.
Nothing had changed.
And yet everything had.
These objects no longer indicated presence or absence clearly.
They lingered.
Like the relationship itself.
She picked up the phone again.
Typed:
“What for?”
Sent.
This time she did not hesitate.
She remained standing.
Waiting.
Not with anxiety.
With attention.
A thought surfaced.
She was no longer willing to let the relationship dissolve by inertia.
Not because she could not.
Because she no longer considered that enough.
The phone vibrated.
“To think.”
Lucía read it once.
Then again.
Think.
The word used to have structure—process, outcome.
Now it did not.
She typed again.
“What if thinking doesn’t change anything?”
Sent.
Silence.
Longer now.
Lucía placed the phone down and sat.
Hands resting on her knees.
She was not angry.
She was not hurt.
She was deciding.
The problem was not Daniel.
It was the structure.
Before, relationships were shaped by forces no one named directly: time, distance, wear.
If one stopped holding, the connection weakened.
If it weakened enough, it ended.
Hard.
But clear.
Now that clarity was gone.
Things could remain suspended, neither moving nor breaking.
That was not a form of love.
It was a state of suspension.
The phone vibrated again.
Lucía did not reach for it immediately.
Then she looked.
“Then we’ll see.”
The response was vague.
Insufficient.
Before, she would have read it as withdrawal.
Now she was not certain.
Lucía stood and walked to the door.
She did not overthink it.
She took her jacket.
Left.
Outside, the air was cold.
The city moved.
She walked toward Daniel’s building.
Not fast.
Not slow.
With direction.
This was not impulse.
It was coherence.
If the limit no longer imposed itself from outside, someone had to define it from within.
She reached the building.
Stopped.
Looked up.
Windows.
Lights.
Continuity.
She breathed.
Not searching for an answer.
For action.
She entered.
Climbed the stairs.
Not out of urgency.
Out of clarity.
She stopped in front of his door.
A pause.
Not long.
Enough.
She raised her hand and knocked.
The sound was simple.
Final.
She waited.
Movement inside.
Lucía held her gaze on the door, not as someone asking permission, but as someone who had decided not to withdraw.
And at that moment, without needing to say it out loud, she understood something she could no longer ignore.
She was not going to let the relationship end simply because the other person stopped holding it.
Not now.
Not in a world where endings no longer carried the same force.
The door began to open.
And with it, something that would not return to its previous form.
