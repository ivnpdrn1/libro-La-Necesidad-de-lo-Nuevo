
Chapter 8 — The Expansion

The video no longer belonged to him.
Mateo understood that on the third day.
He saw it on screens he did not control—at a bar, on a bus, on someone else’s phone—paused at the same frame where the body should have failed and didn’t.
He stopped in front of one of those screens.
Not close.
At a distance.
Three people were watching.
—There. Look how he gets up.
—That’s not real.
—It is. There are multiple angles.
Mateo did not speak.
He did not need to.
The video had detached from him.
He kept walking.
The city had not changed its shape.
It had changed its posture.
He noticed it in small adjustments. A door that closed later than it should. A step that did not stop. A gesture that did not correct.
Nothing extreme.
But enough.
At a corner, someone called his name.
—Mateo.
He turned.
Julián.
Not close.
But known.
—It’s you, Julián said, smiling in a way that was not entirely friendly. The one from the video.
Mateo nodded.
There was no point denying it.
—You’re everywhere.
Mateo did not respond.
Julián looked at him more carefully.
—So, what happened?
—I fell.
Julián laughed.
—I saw that. I mean, what happened?
Mateo hesitated.
—It didn’t work the way it should have.
Julián’s expression shifted.
—Exactly.
Mateo watched him.
—Exactly?
—If that doesn’t work, Julián said, what else doesn’t?
The question remained.
Not as a challenge.
As an opening.
Mateo did not answer immediately.
Julián stepped closer.
—People are talking, he added. Not just about the video. About what it means.
Mateo frowned slightly.
—And what does it mean?
Julián held his gaze.
—That maybe we’ve been obeying things that aren’t as real as we thought.
Something in Mateo recognized that.
No agreement.
Recognition.
—It’s not that simple,” he said.
—Of course it isn’t, Julián replied. But it’s enough.
Mateo looked at him.
That word mattered.
Enough.
They did not finish the conversation.
They did not need to.
The idea was already moving.
That afternoon, Mateo sat in a park.
Not to rest.
To observe.
A group of teenagers ran across the grass.
One of them tripped and fell.
He got up almost immediately.
Laughed.
The others did not stop.
No one asked if he was okay.
The moment passed.
Mateo kept watching.
Not because it was serious.
Because it was not.
A man crossed the street without looking.
The car slowed too late.
No reaction.
Mateo leaned forward, elbows on his knees.
Something was shifting.
Not in the events.
In response to them.
He took out his phone and opened the video.
I watched it without sound.
The fall.
The impact.
He paused it at the same frame.
The break.
Not the fall.
The point where consequence should have appeared clearly—and did not.
Mateo lowered the phone and looked at the park again.
The connection was obvious.
He did not need to explain it.
—Does it bother you?
Mateo looked up.
A woman stood in front of him.
—What?
—Me sitting here.
Mateo shook his head.
—No.
She sat.
Did not look at him immediately.
—I saw you,” she said.
Mateo did not answer.
—The video.
—Yes.
There was a brief silence.
—I fell yesterday too.
Mateo turned.
—And?
She raised her arm.
A bruise.
Nothing serious.
—It should hurt more.
Mateo nodded.
—Yes.
—But it doesn’t.
—No.
She looked at him.
—Is that good?
Mateo hesitated.
—I don’t know.
She held his gaze.
That answer was enough.
—I’m not going to stop doing things because of it.
Mateo did not respond.
But the sentence remained.
She stood and left.
Mateo watched the space she had occupied.
The world was not breaking.
It was loosening.
That night, the video spread again, more widely, more intensely.
But it was no longer just the video.
It was what people were doing with it.
Small tests.
Small risks.
Small deviations.
Not organized.
Not yet.
But increasing.
Mateo saw it everywhere.
Not because he was searching.
Because it had become visible.
He sat by his window.
The city stretched in front of him—same lights, same movement, same appearance of order.
But it is no longer the same system.
He thought of Julián.
The question.
What else doesn’t work?
It was not theoretical.
It was an invitation.
Mateo rested his forehead against the glass and closed his eyes.
Not looking for an answer.
Looking for directions.
When he opened them, they were already there.
Not as a conclusion.
As movement.
If the limit had become negotiable, someone would explore it.
Not to destroy.
To understand.
To test.
To see how far it was.
Mateo picked up his phone.
This time, he did not hesitate.
He wrote a single sentence.
Short.
Unexplained.
He read it once.
Did not change it.
He posted it.
Seconds passed.
Then more.
Responses appeared.
Not all of them understood.
That was not necessary.
Mateo set the phone down and looked at the city again.
No longer as a witness.
As part of it.
He did not know exactly what he had started.
But he knew enough.
This was not about breaking the system.
It was about discovering how far it still functioned.
And that kind of discovery never stops where one intends.
