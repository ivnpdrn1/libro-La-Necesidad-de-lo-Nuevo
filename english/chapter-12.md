
# **Chapter 12 — Andrés: The Unresolved**

Andrés stopped trying to define it too quickly.

That had been his first mistake.

---

He had attempted to give it form—language, structure, a concept that could be explained and contained. But every time he approached it directly, it dissolved into something too broad or too simple.

Neither was accurate.

---

So he changed approach.

---

He began to follow it instead.

---

Not as a theory.

As a behavior.

---

In the classroom, he no longer started from explanation.

He started from observation.

---

—What did you notice today?

---

The question stayed in the room longer than expected.

---

A student answered.

—Nothing unusual.

---

Andrés nodded.

---

—Look again.

---

Silence.

---

Another student spoke.

—People don’t react the same way.

---

Andrés did not confirm it.

---

—To what?

---

The student hesitated.

---

—To things that should matter.

---

Andrés held the answer.

---

—Be specific.

---

The student thought.

---

—A man fell on the street. Nobody stopped.

---

Andrés leaned slightly forward.

---

—Did he get up?

---

—Yes.

---

—Then why should they have stopped?

---

The room shifted.

---

The student frowned.

---

—Because… something happened.

---

Andrés did not interrupt.

---

—Did it?

---

Silence.

---

Another voice entered.

---

—It did, but not enough.

---

Andrés turned.

---

—Not enough for what?

---

—For it to change anything.

---

That was closer.

---

Andrés walked slowly across the room.

---

—We are used to events forcing outcomes, he said. Not always clearly, not always immediately—but enough to guide what comes next.

---

He paused.

---

—What happens if they don’t?

---

No one answered.

---

He let the question remain.

---

—Then we decide, someone said finally.

---

Andrés nodded once.

---

—Yes.

---

The word settled differently.

---

Not as resolution.

As responsibility.

---

The class ended without resistance.

---

That was new.

---

Before, disagreement would have surfaced.

Now…

things remained open.

---

Andrés noticed it.

---

Not only in what was said.

In what was not pushed further.

---

After the class, he did not go home immediately.

He walked.

---

The city did not offer clarity.

---

It confirmed ambiguity.

---

Two people stood arguing.

The argument did not escalate.

Did not resolve.

It faded.

---

A cyclist nearly collided with a pedestrian.

Both adjusted slightly.

Neither reacted.

---

A man dropped something.

Picked it up.

Continued.

---

No interruption.

---

No weight.

---

Andrés stopped at a corner.

---

This was not disorder.

---

It was continuity without consequence.

---

That was harder to address.

---

He resumed walking.

---

A thought followed him.

---

If events no longer forced resolution…

then resolution had to come from somewhere else.

---

But where?

---

Inside?

---

That was unstable.

---

Outside?

---

That required structure.

---

And structure…

imposes.

---

He stopped again.

---

That was the point of tension.

---

Not whether something was happening.

---

But who—or what—would take responsibility for what no longer resolved itself.

---

He reached a café.

Went in.

Sat.

---

Ordered without thinking.

---

The place was quiet.

People spoke.

Softly.

---

No urgency.

---

Even conversations seemed to drift.

---

A couple sat across from each other.

---

—So what do you want to do?

---

The question stayed.

---

—I don’t know.

---

No frustration.

No insistence.

---

Just suspension.

---

Andrés watched them for a moment.

---

Before, that moment would have moved.

Toward agreement.

Or disagreement.

---

Now…

it stayed open.

---

He looked down at the table.

---

This was not a failure of communication.

---

It was a failure of closure.

---

He took a sip of coffee.

---

Cold.

---

He had not noticed when it had cooled.

---

That, too, felt consistent.

---

Time was passing.

---

But not shaping things in the same way.

---

He placed the cup down.

---

If nothing forces a decision…

then indecision becomes stable.

---

And stable indecision…

is not neutral.

---

It accumulates.

---

He stood.

Left money on the table.

---

Walked back outside.

---

The city continued.

---

But it no longer resolved.

---

Andrés stopped one last time before heading home.

---

He looked at the movement around him.

---

Not chaotic.

---

Not ordered.

---

Suspended.

---

He understood something then.

---

Not as a conclusion.

---

As a necessity.

---

If resolution no longer emerges from events…

---

it will have to be imposed.

---

And that…

---

will not be received as neutral.

---

He continued walking.

---

Not faster.

---

But with direction.

---

For the first time, he was no longer only observing the problem.

---

He was approaching the point where observation would no longer be enough.

---
